Part 3 · How We Sell

The SPICED Methodology

At uman, we don't sell software. We sell better sales outcomes. Our sales process follows SPICED — a framework that ensures we qualify deeply, create urgency, and build a strong business case at every stage of the deal.
S
Situation
Understand their reality
Map the prospect's current sales ecosystem. How is their team structured? How many reps? What tools do they use? Are they scaling or optimizing? What does their portfolio look like?
→ Clear understanding of their sales setup and whether uman can create measurable value
P
Pain
Expose inefficiencies
Make sales challenges explicit and acknowledged. Reps only sell what they know. Cross-sell missed. Manual prep eating hours. Portfolio not positioned correctly. No feedback loop.
→ Clearly defined problem that sales leadership recognizes as important to solve
I
Impact
Quantify the business case
Translate inefficiencies into measurable business impact. Time wasted on prep, missed cross-sells, lost win-rate, poor forecasting, slow ramp-up of new reps.
→ Strong financial and strategic argument. Without quantified impact, there is no deal.
C
Critical Event
Why now?
Identify the trigger that forces action. Scaling this quarter? New sales team? Leadership pressure on revenue targets? Sales transformation underway? Deals close because of urgency.
→ Clear timeline tied to a business-critical moment. No critical event = low closing probability.
E
Decision
Navigate the buying process
Drive alignment and close predictably. Map stakeholders (Sales Director, CCO, Head of Sales Ops, C-level). Identify the economic decision-maker and internal champion.
→ Structured buying journey with no surprises. Tailored demos, objection handling, mutual close plan.
+
Implementation
Value realization
Closing is not the finish line. Real impact is. Structured onboarding via workstreams (Strategy, IT setup, Portfolio governance, Per sales team), success milestone tracking, QBRs.
→ Demonstrated ROI and long-term partnership. +10% win-rate (Proximus). +15% cross-sell (Normec).

Part 3 · Sales Enablement

Handling Objections

These are the most common objections you'll encounter. Know them cold — your ability to respond confidently and quickly is what separates good reps from great ones.
We already use ChatGPT / Copilot
Uman is a purpose-built sales enablement brain — not a generic AI. It lives inside your mailbox/CRM, already knows your full service portfolio, pulls in every scrap of internal + external customer intel, and follows your own sales methodology automatically. The result is context-rich, consistent workflows your reps can use instantly. No prompt hacking. No copy-pasting. No scattered guesses.
What about our data security?
Uman is ISO 27001 certified and GDPR compliant, built with enterprise-grade security protocols at every level. Your data stays encrypted, access is role-based, and nothing gets shared without your permission. Security isn't a feature — it's a foundation.
How fast can we go live?
Our implementation plan is optimised for time-to-value — you will be able to be up and running from week 1. Practical · City Guide

Ghent Survival Guide

LEZ
The Low Emission Zone (LEZ)

If you're coming by car, register your license plate BEFORE you drive into the city. Otherwise you'll get an automatic fine — not the best way to kick off your Ghent adventure.

How to register: Go to stad.gent/lez — takes 5 minutes. Do it before your first day.
HOU
Housing

Start looking early. Really early. Ghent fills up fast for short-term rentals. Waiting until the last minute means paying way too much on Airbnb.

Good platforms: ColocHousing, Wing Upkot, ikot.be — start there before anything else.
AFT
After Work

Ghent is a great city. Good food, good bars, good people. The old town is worth exploring. And if you like Belgian beer — you are in the right place.

Tip: Don't just go home after work. The city is part of the experience — enjoy it.
MOV
Getting Around

Ghent is very bike-friendly. Most people cycle everywhere. Public transport works well too. The city center is compact — no car needed day-to-day.

Blue-bike: shared bikes available everywhere — cheap and convenient for the first days.
